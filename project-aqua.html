Project Aqua

Adobe's first creative app for kids, Project Aqua is a free iOS app to inspire kids and parents through activities that grow creative confidence. Built around a world of playful, guided activities, it makes creative expression feel magical, approachable, and confidence-building for kids of all ages.

Key Decisions

Kid-First Navigation

Traditional grids and menus felt too "adult" in testing. We replaced them with an island map, a choose-your-own-adventure entry point that turned browsing into exploration.

Design System Adaptations

Spectrum wasn't built for kids, and early builds felt intimidating. We adapted it with larger touch targets, bolder CTAs, and playful interactions. A hybrid system that enabled 2x team velocity while staying distinctly Adobe.

AI as a Sidekick

Generative tools had to amplify ideas, not replace them. AI shows up as filters that transform drawings into clay, yarn, or origami, keeping the child's work at the center while adding surprise.

Designing for Micro-Delight

Short attention spans meant large feature sets weren't as valuable as moments of surprise. Jellyfish disco loading screens, animated confetti, interactive characters on the map. These went further than any tutorial could.
